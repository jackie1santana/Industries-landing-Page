5 Product/Service Line: Optical Emission Spectroscopy (OES)

Optical Emission Spectroscopy (OES) is a method of Non-destructive Examination that can determine the alloy composition of materials.

Overview: Positive Material Identification (PMI) is a method of Surface Non-Destructive Examination that can determine the alloy composition of materials.

There are several accepted techniques for PMI testing, including PMI, XRF and OES. OES involves generating a spark on a metal’s surface. The energy of the spark causes the electrons in the sample to emit light, which is converted into a spectral pattern. By measuring the intensity of the peaks in this spectrum, the OES analyzer can produce qualitative and quantitative analysis of the material composition.

Although OES is considered a nondestructive testing method, the spark does leave a small burn on the sample surface. OES equipment is not as mobile as PMI or XRF equipment, but it can be used in most locations if preparations are made. OES equipment is capable of measuring Carbon and the lighter elements and can distinguish most grades of carbon steel. Since this equipment does not use radiation, no radiation safety concerns apply.

Top Reasons Why/Occasions When Clients Need this Product/Service:

  1. Find carbon content to be able to calculate Carbon Equivalency
  2. To identify closely related carbon steel grades
  3. Ensure that the material specified in the PO is that used on the job
  4. When facility and inspection staff replace components, they must be able to guarantee that the new part matches required specifications
  5. If a materials certificate is missing
  6. To investigate, post failure, whether the failed equipment met the original materials spec
  7. For turnaround planning purposes, help to ensure that the correct material is available when needed
  8. To verify welding consumables before welding and may be used post welding to ensure that the correct consumables were used
  9. For shutdown welding, can be used to determine the alloys of existing material, allowing the proper match with new material, helping to ensure that an appropriate welding procedure is used
