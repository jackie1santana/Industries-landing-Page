4 Product/Service Line: Positive Materials Identification (PMI)

Service Line Group: Non-destructive Examination (NDE) Service Line: Positive Material Identification (PMI)

Overview: Positive Material Identification (PMI) is a method of Surface Non-Destructive Examination that can determine the alloy composition of materials.

There are several accepted techniques for PMI testing, including PMI, XRF and OES. While technically each of these techniques is PMI, the traditional definition of the term PMI involves interrogation of a metals surface with a beam of radiation produced by a radioactive source. This incident radiation beam interacts with the alloy elements to generate secondary radiation scatter which is measured by the PMI equipment. Each element in the alloy produces a unique and characteristic scatter radiation from which the elements in the alloy can be identified and quantified.

PMI equipment is generally hand held, battery operated, fully mobile, and can be used in most locations. PMI equipment is not capable of measuring Carbon and the lighter elements. Also, traditional methods of generating X-rays have used radioactive isotopes, the use of which requires much documentation and safety considerations.

Top Reasons Why/Occasions When Clients Need this Product/Service:

  1. Ensure that the material specified in the PO is that used on the job
  2. If a materials certificate is missing
  3. When facility and inspection staff replace components, they must be able to guarantee that the new part matches required specifications
  4. To investigate, post failure, whether the failed equipment met the original materials spec
  5. For turnaround planning purposes, help to ensure that the correct material is available when needed
  6. To verify welding consumables before welding and may be used post welding to ensure that the correct consumables were used
  7. For shutdown welding, can be used to determine the alloys of existing material, allowing the proper match with new material, helping to ensure that an appropriate welding procedure is used

Key Questions to Ask to Identify Client Needs:

  1. Do you have any stainless steels or exotic materials on site?
  2. How good do you believe your materials control system is?
  3. Are all of your employees and contractors familiar with your materials segregation policies and color coding standards for material alloys?
  4. Is any of your welding or construction done offsite in shops that may not have adequate receiving and / or material segregation processes?
  5. Are you aware of the potential consequences of using the wrong materials or welding consumables?
